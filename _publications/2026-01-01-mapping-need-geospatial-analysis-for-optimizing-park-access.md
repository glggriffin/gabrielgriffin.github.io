This poster focuses on the geospatial analysis supporting the broader park-access optimization project. It highlights how measures of need are built and why those upstream choices matter when they become inputs to a decision model.

**Contribution:** supported the connection between geospatial need assessment and downstream optimization decisions.

**Method:** used spatially informed measures of access and need to examine how model inputs shape recommendations for park planning.

**Result and impact:** helped communicate that optimization quality depends heavily on how access and need are represented before the solver is ever run.
