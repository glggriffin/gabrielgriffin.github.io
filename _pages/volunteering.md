I value service that helps strengthen student communities, expand professional opportunities, and build organizations that can support others over time. My work with the University of Oklahoma chapter of the Institute of Industrial and Systems Engineers has been one of the clearest ways I have tried to contribute in that spirit.

## Institute of Industrial and Systems Engineers

### Treasurer, 2025-2026

As treasurer of the OU IISE chapter during the 2025-2026 school year, I supported the chapter's operations by helping manage finances and contributing to the planning behind chapter activities. The position required attention to detail, consistency, and a practical understanding of how resources shape what a student organization is able to do well.

I saw that role as more than bookkeeping. Good financial stewardship helps create the conditions for everything else a chapter hopes to accomplish, from professional events to member engagement. Serving as treasurer gave me the chance to help provide that foundation and to support an organization that connects students with one another and with the broader field of industrial and systems engineering.

### President, 2026-2027

For the 2026-2027 school year, I am serving as president of the OU IISE chapter. In this role, I am focused on helping set the chapter's direction, supporting other student leaders, and building a chapter that is both professionally valuable and welcoming to its members. I want the organization to be a place where students can grow as engineers, learn from one another, and develop stronger connections to the profession.

This role has also pushed me to think more deliberately about leadership as a form of service. A strong student chapter depends on planning, reliability, and the willingness to create opportunities that benefit other people. My goal is to help sustain a chapter culture that is thoughtful, organized, and genuinely useful to the students it serves.

Across both positions, my involvement with IISE has taught me that meaningful volunteer work is often quiet and structural. It is not only about holding a title, but about helping build systems, routines, and communities that make it easier for others to participate and succeed.
